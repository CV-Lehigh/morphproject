Consent Form

The purpose of this research project is to examine how one perceives and thinks about social groups. The results from this research may help psychologists better understand how these aspects of human behavior function.
To be eligible to participate in this study, you must be at least 18 years of age.
For this project, you will be asked to look at human faces and make judgments about their age. It will take approximately 45 minutes to complete this experiment. You will receive 1-hour credit toward your CPSY course requirement for participating.
All data will be as anonymous and no identifying information will be collected.
Your responses will be collected on a paper-and-pencil questionnaire and will be entered into a database. The paper and pencil responses will be stored in a locked cabinet. The data will be stored on the researcher’s password-protected computer. This data will be held for five years. Only members of the research team will have access to the data.
There are no anticipated risks associated with participating in this study. However, you may choose not to answer any question or do any task that makes you uncomfortable and you may leave the study at any time. You may also skip any face and continue onto the next one by letting the experimenter know.
Your participation in this study is completely voluntary and you are free to withdraw your permission at any time for any reason without penalty or prejudice from the investigator.
For one’s rights as a research subject, you may contact the following: Office of Research Integrity, Ball State University, Muncie, IN 47306, (765) 285-5070, irb@bsu.edu.
